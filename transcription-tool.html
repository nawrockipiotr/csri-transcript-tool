Transcript Tool

A translation and quality assessment tool for multilingual transcripts, developed at the Centre for Socially Responsible Innovations (CSRI), Faculty of Management, University of Warsaw.

How to use this tool
1. Paste your API key from the selected AI provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google). You only need to do this once if you choose to save it.
2. Choose a mode: Translation converts the transcript into your target language, Quality Assessment checks the transcript for errors and flags them, or Both does both at once.
3. Drag & drop your transcript files (TXT, DOCX, or SRT) or click to browse.
4. Click Process Files and wait for results. You can stop at any time.
5. Export results as SRT (for subtitles), TXT (clean transcript), or DOCX.
How to read quality flags
In Quality Assessment mode, the tool highlights potential transcription errors directly in the text. Flags do not mean the text is wrong — they mark places worth verifying.
innovashun Minor — garbled but understandable from context (e.g. ASR mishearing, informal speech, slight distortion)
Johsen Mrak Serious — incomprehensible, scrambled names, missing speech, or nonsensical fragments that need human verification
About the Summary option
When enabled, the tool generates a structured overview of the transcript: context, main topics, key findings, and ambiguities. The summary does not attribute statements to specific speakers (labels can be unreliable) and does not quote the transcript. It is prepended to TXT and DOCX exports but excluded from SRT files (which are for subtitles only).
